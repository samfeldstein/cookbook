1. Divide **1 1/2 lbs ground beef** into **8 equal portions (3 oz each)**. Cover and refrigerate until ready to cook.[^1]
2. Stir together **1/3 c mayo** and **1 tsp mustard**. Set aside.
3. Butter **8 brioche buns** and toast on a griddle or skillet over medium heat until golden brown. Transfer to a rack.
4. Increase heat to medium-high. Place beef portions on the hot surface. Cover each with parchment paper and smash into thin patties. Remove parchment, season with **salt**, **pepper**, and **garlic powder**, add a dab of mayo sauce, and flip. Sear about 2 minutes on the first side and 1 minute on the second side.
5. Place **cheese slices** on half the patties, top with a second patty to make doubles, then remove from heat.
6. Place patties on toasted buns with prepared toppings.

## Notes

Original recipe from [Natasha's Kitchen](https://natashaskitchen.com/smash-burger-recipe/).

[^1]: Cold fat has structure, so the burger holds its shape. Also stays better locked inside the muscle fibers, preserving flavor.
