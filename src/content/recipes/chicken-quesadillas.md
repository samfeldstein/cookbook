1. Whisk **2 Tbsp vegetable oil**, **2 crushed garlic cloves**, **1 tsp oregano**, **1 Tbsp cumin**, **1 tsp cayenne**, **1 tsp paprika**, **1 tsp white sugar**, **juice of 1 lime**, **1 Tbsp olive oil**, **1 tsp kosher salt**, and **1 tsp freshly ground pepper**.
2. Cut **3 chicken breasts** in half horizontally. Pound them flat as you can.
3. Massage chicken breast in the marinade to coat evenly.
4. Cover and refrigerate at least 30 minutes.
5. Preheat a skillet over high heat.
6. Cook marinated chicken on both sides until nicely golden brown and cooked through.
7. Remove from skillet, let cool slightly, then slice.
8. Fill **flour tortillas** with sliced chicken, **Monterey Jack cheese**, and **sharp cheddar cheese**, then fold into quesadillas.
9. Heat a generous amount of vegetable oil in a skillet over medium heat.
10. Cook quesadillas till golden brown on each side.
11. Serve with **guac**, **salsa**, and **sour cream**.

## Notes

Cutting breasts in half and pounding them helps them cook evenly.

Chicken very smoky, though maybe because I was using a steel skillet. Try in the oven next time.

Instead of big tortillas folded over, I like to use two small tortillas like a sandwich. Sonora-style are nice, they crisp up fast.
